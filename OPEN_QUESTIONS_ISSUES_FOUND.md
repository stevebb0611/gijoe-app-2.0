# 22 Dashboard Stats — "Complete" doesn't reflect duplicate whole copies
Issue Found (July 2026): asked whether owning 2 complete copies of the same figure (e.g. Firefly) counts twice toward the "Complete" KPI (e.g. "74/117"). It does not. Both the numerator and denominator of that stat are counted at the unique-figure level, not per-instance: `JoeData.totals()` (`web/src/store.js:171-176`) builds a `Set` of owned `catalogId`s (117 = unique figures), then for each unique id increments `complete` once if *any* owned copy is whole (`s.whole > 0`) — so a figure with 2 whole copies still only contributes +1. "Total Figures" is the only KPI that counts raw instances (218-style, duplicates included); "Unique Figures" and "Complete" both operate on the unique-figure set. Confirmed identical semantics in the original prototype (`wf-data.jsx:223,239-249`) — not a Vite-port bug, original design intent (or oversight) carried through.
Holding for now — owner has not decided whether this is the intended semantic or whether a distinct "whole copies" stat (would count every complete instance, duplicates included) should be added alongside it.
